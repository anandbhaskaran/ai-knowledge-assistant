# Editorial Guidelines

## Voice & Tone
**Intelligent, confident, conversational**

We're authoritative but not condescending. We provide analytical depth without academic stuffiness. We take clear positions while acknowledging complexity.

**Avoid:** Hype, hyperbole, breathless urgency, jargon-heavy writing, talking down to readers.

---

## Writing Standards

**Clarity First**
- Write for intelligent non-specialists
- Explain jargon on first use
- Use concrete examples
- Average 15-20 words per sentence
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)

**Structure**
- Headlines: Clear, specific, 60-80 characters
- Intro: Hook + why it matters now (100-150 words)
- Body: One idea per paragraph, subheadings every 3-4 paragraphs
- Conclusion: Synthesize insights, don't just restate

**Support Your Claims**
- Cite credible sources
- Provide context for data
- Distinguish facts from analysis
- Present multiple perspectives on controversial topics

---

## Topic Guidelines

**Geopolitics:** Explain context, consider multiple stakeholders, avoid good/bad framing, acknowledge uncertainty

**Macroeconomics:** Explain mechanisms, use analogies, acknowledge competing theories, connect to real-world impacts

**Cryptocurrency:** Explain tech when relevant, present bull and bear cases, avoid shilling/FUD, define crypto jargon

**Technology:** Focus on implications, consider second-order effects, question hype, explain technical concepts clearly

---

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing:
- [ ] Thesis is clear and defendable
- [ ] Claims are supported with evidence
- [ ] Technical terms are explained
- [ ] A smart non-specialist can understand it
- [ ] No typos or grammatical errors
- [ ] The "so what?" is obvious

---

## We Don't Publish

- Clickbait or sensationalism
- Unverified rumors as fact
- Trading/investment advice
- Conspiracy theories without evidence
- Purely reactive news without analysis
- Partisan cheerleading

---

**When in doubt:** Choose clarity over cleverness, accuracy over speed, honesty over hype.
